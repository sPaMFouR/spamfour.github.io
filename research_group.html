Research Group

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Oskar Klein Centre (OKC), Department of Astronomy, Stockholm University. I work on the observational investigation and hydrodynamical modelling of core collapse supernovae. My research also involves understanding the mass-loss history of the progenitor star directly connecting them to evolution of massive stars. I work in the supernova group headed by Jesper Sollerman who is a professor at the Department of Astronomy, Stockholm University, Sweden.

Supernova Group, Oskar Klein Centre

The supernova group at the Oskar Klein Centre works on the structure, dynamics, and nucleosynthesis of core-collapse supernovae; the interaction of supernova ejecta with circumstellar material, which provides direct information about pre-explosion mass loss; the physics of superluminous supernovae and other rare transients; and the electromagnetic counterparts to gravitational-wave events from compact-object mergers. OKC is an active member of several international collaborations in time-domain and multi-messenger astronomy, including GROWTH, ZTF, ePESSTO+, ENGRAVE, and LS4. These collaborations coordinate high-cadence wide-field surveys with prompt spectroscopic classification and deep multi-wavelength follow-up, enabling systematic studies of young supernovae, rare transients, and kilonovae.

Past Affiliations

Before joining Stockholm in 2024, I was a Fixed-Term Assistant Professor at the Hiroshima Astrophysical Science Center, Hiroshima University, Japan (2021–2024), where I led follow-up programs on the 1.5m Kanata and 3.8m Seimei telescopes under J-GEM, and worked with Prof. Koji Kawabata and Prof. Keiichi Maeda. Previously I was a Research Associate–I at ARIES, Nainital, India (2020–2021). My Ph.D. was completed in 2020 at the Indian Institute of Science / Indian Institute of Astrophysics, Bengaluru, under Prof. G. C. Anupama, and I continue to collaborate closely with colleagues at IIA, IUCAA, and ARIES on supernova science.
